Study Abroad Intakes

There is a significant influx of students each year in universities located abroad. Therefore, universities offer multiple intakes to meet the number of students enrolled each semester. Universities have several intakes to provide students more freedom in selecting the time they want to apply for admission and start their studies

Fall Intake

  • The fall intake starts in late August or early September and ends in the latter part of December. Fall admissions are available at almost all universities, including prestigious ones like MIT, Harvard, Stanford, etc
  • It attracts the most applications since many high school or undergraduate students' academic years end in April, making fall admission a popular choice for many students.
  • There is greater competition and slim admission prospects since more applicants for the fall intake. Comparatively speaking, the acceptance rate is lower than the spring intake.
  • Most institutional awards are given out on a first-come, first-served basis. For students applying for the fall intake, the university has additional scholarship funding available.

Summer Intake

  • The summer intake is a unique situation provided by a few universities to a select group of students for certain courses, primarily diplomas or other summer courses.
  • There are more opportunities to receive an offer because institutions accepting applications for the summer intake receive fewer applicants.
  • Fewer students apply to universities offering admission during the Summer intake, which means that there are greater chances of getting an offer.
  • Given the ongoing disruptions caused by COVID-19, students from across the world wishing to pursue higher education in the world have been affected and are also more likely to face even more competition in admission, which makes applying for the Summer intake 2021 considerably beneficial.

Spring Intake

  • The spring intake starts in January and ends by the beginning of May. There are a few universities that may not offer the Spring intake, so you might need to inquire with your preferred university.c
  • The competition in the spring intake is less as not many students prefer to opt for it. Less students apply in spring and therefore the acceptance rate tends to be higher than the fall intake.
  • Mostly, students who go for the spring intake are the ones who have an internship or a job to pursue after their graduation, and require more time to gain relevant experience and settle down nicely.
  • Universities might have lesser funds available to administer scholarships in Spring. Also, as the students will be beginning with their studies in the middle of the academic year, less on-campus opportunities are available for the spring/winter students.

Difference Between Fall and Spring Intake

Factors Fall intake Spring intake
Universites Almost all universities offer fall intake. There are a few universities that might not offer spring intake.
Acceptance rate More students apply in fall thus, the acceptance rate is lower. Acceptance rate is comparatively higher.
Job opportunites More summer internship opportunities. Less on-campus job opportunities.
Scholarships More scholarship funds available. Less funds to administer scholarships.
Preparation Indian students typically graduate in May, which leaves them very little time to get ready. Indian students are given more time to make the necessary preparations in order to enroll in the university of their choosing.
